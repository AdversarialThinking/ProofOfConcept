# Cyber-Criminology & Behavioral Analysis

## Overview

Technical vulnerabilities are often just the "how" of a security breach. This directory focuses on the **"why."** As part of my specilization in Technical GRC and the human element, I utilize these case studies to deconstruct the psychological and criminological frameworks that drive social engineering and insider threats. By understanding the behavioral mechanics of an attack, we can build more resilient, human-centric defense-in-depth strategies.

## Core Research Areas

* **Victim-Offender Overlap:** Analayzing how victimization leads to the internalization of offensive tactics.
* **Cognitive Security:** Studying how attackers manipulate trust and bypass human judgement.
* **Prosocial Governance:** Implementing community-driven cultures to mitigate risk.
* **Insider Threat Modeling:** Identifying behavioral markers and systemic gaps before they are exploited.

## The "CISO-in-a-Box Approach

These studies directly inform my **CISO-in-a-Box** business model. My philosophy is that **technical debt is patched with code, but psychological debt is patched with culture.** Effective Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) must account for the unpredictability of human behavior. I am dedicated to bridging this gap through empirical research and technical hardening.
